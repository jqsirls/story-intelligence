## Risk Management

### User Acceptance Risks

**Risk**: Users hate emails (unsubscribe spike)  
**Mitigation**: 
- Veto authority (30-50% emails vetoed for insufficient signal)
- Frequency caps (max 1 insight/day)
- Quiet hours respect
- Kill switches (instant disable)
- **Rollback**: Flip kill switch within 5 minutes

**Risk**: Therapeutic overreach (parent backlash)  
**Mitigation**:
- Clinical review mandatory
- Parent opt-in required
- Never diagnose
- Professional referral always
- **Rollback**: Disable therapeutic pipeline, offer refund

### Revenue Risks

**Risk**: Referral reward costs exceed projections  
**Mitigation**:
- Track cost per acquisition
- Adjust rewards if CAC too high
- Cap rewards per user
- **Threshold**: If CAC >$50, reduce rewards

**Risk**: Credit fraud (fake referrals)  
**Mitigation**:
- Verify email on signup
- Credit only on subscription (not signup)
- 90-day expiry
- Monitor for patterns
- **Action**: Flag suspicious patterns, manual review

## Questions & Answers

### Q: Why not send all emails? Wouldn't that increase engagement?

**A**: Short-term maybe, long-term no. Users unsubscribe from noise. Veto authority earns trust. 40% open rate with 50% veto rate beats 25% open rate with 0% veto rate.

### Q: Why comparative vs absolute scoring?

**A**: Users don't understand "82/100". They understand "5 minutes faster than usual". Comparative is actionable, absolute is storage.

### Q: Why clinical review for therapeutic features? Competitors don't do this.

**A**: Liability protection, trust, credibility. We're suggesting clinical pathways to vulnerable populations. Can't afford to get this wrong.

### Q: What if referral costs exceed projections?

**A**: We track CAC per referral. If CAC >$50, we reduce reward amounts. Currently tracking $15-20 CAC, comfortable margin.

### Q: How do we measure "silence as feature"?

**A**: Veto rate, open rate, unsubscribe rate. Healthy veto rate (30-50%) + high open rate (40%) = earning trust.
