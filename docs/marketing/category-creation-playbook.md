# Category Creation Playbook

**Last Updated**: 2025-12-14  
**Audience**: Marketing | Sales | All Communicators | Product  
**Status**: Production Ready

## Overview

This document establishes clear language for positioning Storytailor as a NEW category, not "better AI stories." The goal is to create a distinct category that doesn't compete in the "AI story app" space, but instead defines a new family experience category.

## The Category Definition

### What We Are NOT

**NOT Books:**
- We're not competing with reading experiences
- We're not replacing bedtime stories
- We're not a library or book collection
- We're not publishing or printing

**NOT Reading Apps:**
- We're not a digital library
- We're not an e-reader
- We're not a story collection
- We're not passive consumption

**NOT AI Story Generators:**
- We're not "AI-powered stories"
- We're not template-based personalization
- We're not automated content generation
- We're not generic AI content

**NOT Publishing:**
- We're not a publishing company
- We're not printing books
- We're not distributing commercially
- We're not competing with authors/illustrators

### What We ARE

**IS: Story Creation Experience + Real-World Activity Extension**

**The Category:**
- **Story creation platform** (primary)
- **Family storytelling experience** (secondary)
- **Interactive story co-creation** (tertiary)
- **Story-to-activity bridge** (unique differentiator)

**Key Characteristics:**
- Active creation (not passive consumption)
- Co-creation with child (not just for child)
- Real-world activity extension (not just digital)
- Family bonding experience (not just entertainment)

## Category Analogies

### Analogy 1: "LEGO is to Architecture as Storytailor is to Books"

**The Analogy:**
- LEGO doesn't compete with architecture magazines
- LEGO doesn't replace looking at buildings
- LEGO is about BUILDING, not appreciating
- Storytailor is about CREATING, not reading

**How to Use:**
- "Storytailor is to books what LEGOs are to looking at architecture. We're not replacing the reading experience - we're adding a BUILD experience. Just like LEGOs don't compete with architecture magazines, we don't compete with books. We're creation time, not consumption time."

**Key Points:**
- Different jobs to be done
- Complementary, not competitive
- Both have value
- Different experiences

### Analogy 2: "Art Studio vs. Art Museum"

**The Analogy:**
- Art museum = appreciating masterpieces (passive)
- Art studio = making your own art (active)
- Both matter, both are valuable
- One doesn't replace the other

**How to Use:**
- "We're the art studio, they're the art museum. Both matter. One is about appreciating masterpieces, one is about making your own. Storytailor is creation time - you and your child create stories together. Books are appreciation time - you read beautiful stories together. Both are valuable family experiences."

**Key Points:**
- Creation vs. consumption
- Active vs. passive
- Both valuable
- Different experiences

### Analogy 3: "Cooking at Home vs. Eating at Restaurants"

**The Analogy:**
- Restaurants = appreciating great food (consumption)
- Cooking at home = creating meals together (creation)
- Both are valuable experiences
- One doesn't replace the other

**How to Use:**
- "Storytailor is like cooking at home with your child - you create something together. Books are like eating at a great restaurant - you appreciate something beautiful together. Both are wonderful family experiences, but they're different. We're creation time, they're consumption time."

**Key Points:**
- Creation vs. consumption
- Process vs. product
- Both valuable
- Different experiences

## What to Call Us

### Primary Language

**"Story creation platform"**
- Emphasizes creation, not consumption
- Positions as platform, not app
- Focuses on active experience
- Avoids "AI" or "story generator"

**Usage:**
- "Storytailor is a story creation platform"
- "Our story creation platform enables families to..."
- "The story creation experience includes..."

### Secondary Language

**"Family storytelling experience"**
- Emphasizes family bonding
- Focuses on experience, not product
- Highlights co-creation
- Positions as activity, not content

**Usage:**
- "A family storytelling experience"
- "Create stories together as a family"
- "Family story creation time"

### Language to AVOID

**❌ "AI-powered stories"**
- Positions us IN the AI category
- Focuses on technology, not experience
- Competes with "AI story apps"
- Not our category

**❌ "Personalized stories"**
- Too generic
- Could mean template customization
- Doesn't differentiate
- Doesn't emphasize creation

**❌ "Story generator"**
- Sounds automated
- Doesn't emphasize co-creation
- Focuses on output, not process
- Not our positioning

**❌ "AI story app"**
- Positions us WITH competitors
- Focuses on technology
- Doesn't differentiate
- Not our category

### Language to USE

**✅ "Powered by Story Intelligence"**
- Our proprietary term
- Emphasizes methodology, not tech
- Differentiates from "AI-powered"
- Positions as craft system

**✅ "Story creation with your child"**
- Emphasizes co-creation
- Focuses on experience
- Highlights family bonding
- Avoids technology focus

**✅ "Story creation experience"**
- Emphasizes experience
- Focuses on process
- Highlights interaction
- Avoids product positioning

## What to Call Competitors

### Language Rules

**❌ DON'T Say:**
- "Other AI story companies" (positions us WITH them)
- "AI story apps" (positions us IN that category)
- "AI-powered competitors" (focuses on technology)
- "Other personalized story services" (too generic)

**✅ DO Say:**
- "Automated content generation" (describes their method)
- "Template-based personalization" (describes their approach)
- "Generic story apps" (describes their output)
- "Basic story customization" (describes their limitation)

### Competitive Positioning Language

**When Comparing:**
- "Most story apps use automated content generation"
- "Template-based personalization services"
- "Generic story apps that insert names into templates"
- "Basic story customization tools"

**Key Principle:**
- Never mention "AI" when describing competitors
- Focus on their method (automation, templates)
- Focus on their limitation (generic, basic)
- Never position us WITH them

## Job Creation vs. Job Replacement Framing

### The Core Message

**"We're not taking storytelling jobs - we're creating millions of storytelling OPPORTUNITIES."**

### Job Creation Framing

**What We Create:**
- Millions of storytelling opportunities
- Every child becomes a storyteller
- Generation of authors, not end of authors
- More storytelling, not less

**Language:**
- "We're creating millions of storytelling opportunities"
- "Every child becomes a storyteller"
- "Not the end of authors, the beginning of a generation of authors"
- "We're not taking jobs, we're creating opportunities"

## Language Enforcement Rules

### When to Use "Story Intelligence"

**USE "Story Intelligence" When:**
- Describing our technology/methodology
- Differentiating from competitors
- Explaining how we create stories
- Positioning our approach

**Examples:**
- "Powered by Story Intelligence"
- "Story Intelligence creates award-caliber stories"
- "Our Story Intelligence methodology"
- "Story Intelligence crafts narratives"

### When NOT to Use Generic Tech Terms

**DON'T Say:**
- "AI-powered"
- "Machine learning"
- "Artificial intelligence"
- "Algorithm-based"

**Why:**
- Positions us IN the AI category
- Focuses on technology, not craft
- Competes with "AI story apps"
- Not our differentiation

### How to Describe Technology WITHOUT Saying "AI"

**Instead of "AI," Say:**
- "Story Intelligence methodology"
- "Proprietary storytelling framework"
- "Award-winning author's craft systematized"
- "Intelligent understanding system"

**Examples:**
- ❌ "Our AI creates personalized stories"
- ✅ "Story Intelligence crafts award-caliber narratives"

- ❌ "AI-powered story generation"
- ✅ "Story Intelligence methodology creates stories"

- ❌ "Advanced AI technology"
- ✅ "Proprietary Story Intelligence framework"

### Positioning as Methodology/Craft System

**Key Language:**
- "Story Intelligence is JQ's storytelling methodology"
- "Award-winning author's craft systematized"
- "Proprietary framework for narrative creation"
- "Literary craft system, not tech stack"

**Why This Works:**
- Emphasizes craft, not technology
- Positions as expertise, not automation
- Differentiates from "AI story apps"
- Builds credibility through creator story
