## Overview

This document provides clear guidelines for handling criticism, tough questions, and sensitive topics on social media. The goal is to maintain brand voice, build trust, and handle difficult conversations with grace and professionalism.

## When to Talk About AI (and When NOT To)

### Proactive Mentions: Only in Transparency/Education Content

**When to Proactively Mention:**
- Educational blog posts about AI ethics
- Transparency content about our practices
- Thought leadership on responsible AI use
- Educational content about our methodology

**Language:**
- "We use OpenAI's GPT models as foundation technology"
- "Story Intelligence is my methodology executed using foundation models"
- "The ethics of AI training data is an evolving question"
- "I focus on what I can control: my methodology, output, transparency"

**Platforms:**
- Blog (long-form thought leadership)
- LinkedIn (professional transparency)
- YouTube (educational content)
- Email (educational newsletters)

### Reactive Mentions: When Directly Asked

**When Asked: "What AI do you use?"**

**Answer:**
"We use OpenAI's GPT models as foundation technology, combined with our proprietary Story Intelligence methodology developed by award-winning author JQ Sirls. Story Intelligence is his storytelling framework - like a master chef creating a cookbook of techniques."

**When Asked: "Is this AI-generated?"**

**Answer:**
"We use Story Intelligence‚Ñ¢ - our proprietary technology that combines narrative mastery, child development expertise, and therapeutic insights. It's not just AI generating content; it's intelligent understanding that creates award-caliber stories uniquely tailored to each child."

**Key Points:**
- Acknowledge foundation model use
- Emphasize our original methodology
- Position as tools, not the product
- Reference JQ's credentials

### Never Lead With: "AI-Powered" or Technology Descriptions

**What NOT to Lead With:**
- "AI-powered stories"
- "Artificial intelligence creates..."
- "Machine learning generates..."
- "Advanced AI technology"

**Why:**
- Positions us IN the AI category
- Focuses on technology, not experience
- Competes with "AI story apps"
- Not our differentiation

### Always Lead With: Outcomes, Creator Story, Methodology

**What to Lead With:**
- "Create award-caliber stories with your child"
- "Award-winning author JQ Sirls' storytelling methodology"
- "Story creation experience powered by Story Intelligence"
- "Family storytelling platform"

**Why:**
- Focuses on value, not technology
- Emphasizes creator credibility
- Positions as experience, not product
- Differentiates from competitors

- Position as complementary

**Tone:**
- Confident
- Educational
- Not defensive
- Visionary

## Engagement Decision Tree

### Respond If: Genuine Question, Misconception to Clarify, Opportunity to Educate

**Genuine Questions:**
- "How does this work?"
- "Is this safe for children?"
- "What makes this different?"
- "How is this ethical?"

**Misconceptions to Clarify:**
- "This is just AI slop"
- "This replaces authors"
- "This steals from artists"
- "This is bad for children"

**Opportunities to Educate:**
- Questions about storytelling
- Interest in our methodology
- Curiosity about quality
- Engagement with our mission

**Response Approach:**
- Acknowledge the question/concern
- Provide accurate information
- Reference relevant documentation
- Invite further discussion if appropriate

### Ignore If: Trolling, Bad Faith, No Productive Outcome Possible

**Trolling Indicators:**
- Repetitive attacks
- Personal attacks
- No genuine engagement
- Spam or irrelevant

**Bad Faith Indicators:**
- Refusing to engage with information
- Moving goalposts
- Attacking, not discussing
- No interest in understanding

**No Productive Outcome:**
- Already explained multiple times
- Will not accept any answer
- Only interested in attacking
- No possibility of understanding

**When to Ignore:**
- After one attempt to engage
- If response is clearly trolling
- If no productive outcome possible
- If escalates to personal attacks

### Escalate If: Legal Concern, Safety Issue, PR Crisis Potential

**Legal Concerns:**
- Copyright claims
- Trademark issues
- Legal threats
- Regulatory questions

**Safety Issues:**
- Child safety concerns
- Content safety issues
- Privacy concerns
- Security threats

**PR Crisis Potential:**
- Widespread misinformation
- Viral negative content
- Media attention
- Industry impact

**Escalation Process:**
1. Assess severity immediately
2. Consult legal/PR if needed
3. Prepare coordinated response
4. Engage appropriately
5. Follow up and monitor

## Crisis Communication Plan

### What Constitutes a "Crisis" on Social

**Crisis Indicators:**
- Viral negative content
- Widespread misinformation
- Media attention
- Legal concerns raised
- Safety issues mentioned
- Industry impact
- Significant engagement spike (negative)

**Not a Crisis:**
- Individual criticism
- Isolated negative comments
- Standard questions
- Normal engagement

### Immediate Response Protocol (Within 1 Hour)

**Step 1: Assess (0-15 minutes)**
- Determine if it's a crisis
- Assess severity and reach
- Identify key concerns
- Check for legal/safety issues

**Step 2: Consult (15-30 minutes)**
- Legal if needed
- PR if needed
- Leadership if needed
- Relevant experts

**Step 3: Prepare (30-45 minutes)**
- Draft response
- Get approvals
- Prepare supporting materials
- Plan follow-up

**Step 4: Respond (45-60 minutes)**
- Post initial response
- Acknowledge concern
- Provide information
- Invite further discussion

### Who Approves Responses

**Standard Responses:**
- Social media manager
- Marketing lead
- Community manager

**Crisis Responses:**
- Marketing lead
- PR/Communications
- Legal (if needed)
- Leadership (if severe)

**Escalation Responses:**
- Leadership
- Legal
- PR/Communications
- External counsel (if needed)

### When to Take Offline

**Take Offline If:**
- Complex issue requiring detailed discussion
- Privacy concerns
- Legal matters
- Personal information involved
- Escalating conflict

**How to Take Offline:**
- "I'd be happy to discuss this further. Can you DM me or email support@storytailor.com?"
- "This is important - let's continue this conversation privately. [Contact method]"
- "I want to make sure we address this properly. Let's connect directly."

### Follow-Up Procedures

**After Initial Response:**
- Monitor engagement
- Respond to follow-up questions
- Provide additional information if needed
- Update internal team

**After Resolution:**
- Document the issue
- Review response effectiveness
- Update guidelines if needed
- Share learnings with team
