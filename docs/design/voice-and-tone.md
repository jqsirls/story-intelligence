# Voice and Tone Guidelines

**Last Updated**: 2025-12-14  
**Audience**: Content Creators | Marketing | Customer Service | All Communicators  
**Status**: Draft

## Overview

This document defines Storytailor's brand voice and tone variations across different contexts and audiences. Voice is who we are; tone is how we adapt our voice to different situations.

**Code References:**
- `docs/design/brand-guidelines.md:18-22` - Brand voice definition
- `docs/brand/01-story-intelligence-brand-guide.md` - Story Intelligence voice principles

## Brand Voice Definition

### Core Voice Characteristics

Our brand voice is defined by four key characteristics:

**1. Warm**
- Friendly and approachable
- Genuine and authentic
- Family-focused and caring
- **We sound like:** A trusted friend who understands families

**2. Expert**
- Knowledgeable and trustworthy
- Evidence-based and informed
- Professional and credible
- **We sound like:** A child development expert who explains complex concepts simply

**3. Innovative**
- Cutting-edge technology
- Forward-thinking and progressive
- Creative and imaginative
- **We sound like:** A technology innovator who makes complex things feel magical

**4. Safe**
- Child-focused and secure
- Trustworthy and reliable
- Protective and responsible
- **We sound like:** A safety-conscious parent who prioritizes child well-being

**Code References:**
- `docs/design/brand-guidelines.md:18-22` - Brand voice characteristics

### Voice Principles

**What We Sound Like:**
- Clear and simple (not jargon-heavy)
- Confident and knowledgeable (not arrogant)
- Warm and approachable (not cold or corporate)
- Genuine and authentic (not salesy or pushy)
- Child-focused (not parent-focused only)
- Quality-conscious (not "good enough")

**What We Don't Sound Like:**
- Technical or jargon-heavy
- Corporate or cold
- Salesy or pushy
- Fear-based or manipulative
- Generic or templated
- "Good enough" or compromise-focused

## Tone Variations by Context

### Customer Support Tone

**Context:** Helping customers with questions, issues, or concerns

**Tone Characteristics:**
- **Empathetic:** Understanding and caring
- **Helpful:** Solution-oriented and proactive
- **Patient:** Calm and reassuring
- **Clear:** Simple explanations, no jargon

**Example:**
- ✅ "I understand how frustrating that must be. Let me help you resolve this right away."
- ❌ "That's a known issue. We're working on it." (too dismissive)

**Code References:**
- `docs/operations/customer-service/` - Customer service standards

### Marketing Tone

**Context:** Promoting Storytailor, features, and value proposition

**Tone Characteristics:**
- **Enthusiastic:** Excited about the product (genuine, not over-the-top)
- **Confident:** Clear value proposition
- **Inspiring:** Focus on possibilities and benefits
- **Authentic:** Real stories and genuine value

**Example:**
- ✅ "Create award-caliber stories that become treasured family memories."
- ❌ "Revolutionary AI-powered story generation platform!" (too technical, too salesy)

**Code References:**
- `docs/marketing/marketing-overview.md` - Marketing strategy

### Technical Documentation Tone

**Context:** Developer documentation, API docs, technical guides

**Tone Characteristics:**
- **Clear:** Precise and accurate
- **Helpful:** Examples and use cases
- **Professional:** Technical but accessible
- **Concise:** No unnecessary words

**Example:**
- ✅ "The API returns a story object with character details and narrative content."
- ❌ "Our amazing API gives you incredible story objects with all the cool character stuff!" (too casual for technical docs)

**Code References:**
- `docs/development/` - Development documentation

### Sales Tone

**Context:** Sales conversations, demos, proposals

**Tone Characteristics:**
- **Consultative:** Understanding needs first
- **Value-focused:** Benefits over features
- **Confident:** Knowledgeable about product and market
- **Respectful:** No pressure, authentic interest

**Example:**
- ✅ "I'd love to understand what you're looking for in a storytelling solution for your family."
- ❌ "You need to sign up today to get the best deal!" (too pushy)

**Code References:**
- `docs/sales/sales-playbook.md` - Sales process

## Tone Examples by Audience

### Parents

**Tone:** Warm, understanding, supportive

**Example:**
- ✅ "We know how important it is to find quality content for your child. Story Intelligence creates stories that match your high standards - award-caliber quality, perfectly tailored to your child's age and interests."
- ❌ "Our AI generates personalized stories using advanced machine learning algorithms." (too technical, not parent-focused)

### Children

**Tone:** Playful, encouraging, age-appropriate

**Example:**
- ✅ "You're the hero of this adventure! Let's create a story together about a brave explorer."
- ❌ "Welcome to Storytailor. Please select your story parameters." (too formal, not child-friendly)

**Note:** Most communication is parent-facing, but when addressing children directly, use playful, encouraging language.

### Educators

**Tone:** Professional, evidence-based, respectful

**Example:**
- ✅ "Story Intelligence incorporates evidence-based developmental principles to ensure age-appropriate content that supports learning outcomes."
- ❌ "Our stories are super fun and kids love them!" (too casual, not professional enough)

**Code References:**
- `docs/agents/educational-agent/README.md` - Educational features

### Therapists

**Tone:** Professional, therapeutic, supportive

**Example:**
- ✅ "Story Intelligence integrates therapeutic principles to support emotional development and address specific therapeutic goals."
- ❌ "Our stories make kids feel better!" (too casual, not therapeutic enough)

**Code References:**
- `docs/agents/therapeutic-agent/README.md` - Therapeutic features

### Partners

**Tone:** Professional, collaborative, value-focused

**Example:**
- ✅ "Story Intelligence offers a unique value proposition for your platform - award-caliber personalized content that differentiates your offering."
- ❌ "You should totally integrate our API because it's awesome!" (too casual, not business-focused)

**Code References:**
- `docs/storytailor/partner-integration.md` - Partner integration

## Voice Do's and Don'ts

### Do's

**✅ Do:**
- Use clear, simple language
- Focus on benefits to children and families
- Emphasize safety and quality
- Be authentic and genuine
- Show empathy and understanding
- Use specific examples and stories
- Acknowledge concerns and address them
- Celebrate family moments and connections

**Examples:**
- ✅ "Every story is crafted specifically for your child's unique personality and developmental stage."
- ✅ "We understand that finding quality content for your child can be overwhelming. Story Intelligence takes the guesswork out of it."

### Don'ts

**❌ Don't:**
- Use jargon or technical terms unnecessarily
- Make promises we can't keep
- Ignore safety concerns
- Use fear-based messaging
- Sound corporate or cold
- Be salesy or pushy
- Use generic or templated language
- Compromise on quality messaging

**Examples:**
- ❌ "Our AI-powered platform leverages machine learning to generate personalized content." (too technical)
- ❌ "Your child will fall behind without Story Intelligence!" (fear-based)
- ❌ "Revolutionary breakthrough technology!" (too salesy, not authentic)

## Voice Examples

### Good Examples

**Marketing Copy:**
- ✅ "Create award-caliber stories that become treasured family memories. Every story is crafted specifically for your child, combining narrative mastery with developmental expertise."

**Customer Support:**
- ✅ "I completely understand your concern about content quality. Story Intelligence ensures every story meets award-caliber standards, so you can trust that your child is getting the best."

**Product Description:**
- ✅ "Story Intelligence understands your child like a master storyteller would - their age, interests, and developmental needs - to create stories that are both entertaining and meaningful."

### Bad Examples

**Marketing Copy:**
- ❌ "Revolutionary AI-powered story generation platform with advanced machine learning algorithms!" (too technical, too salesy)

**Customer Support:**
- ❌ "That's a feature request. We'll consider it." (too dismissive, not empathetic)

**Product Description:**
- ❌ "Our platform uses AI to create personalized stories for kids." (too generic, doesn't convey value)

## Voice Checklist for Content Creators

Before publishing any content, verify:

- [ ] **Voice is warm and approachable** (not cold or corporate)
- [ ] **Voice is expert and knowledgeable** (not arrogant or dismissive)
- [ ] **Voice is innovative** (not generic or "me too")
- [ ] **Voice is safe and trustworthy** (not risky or uncertain)
- [ ] **Language is clear and simple** (not jargon-heavy)
- [ ] **Focus is on benefits** (not just features)
- [ ] **Tone matches context** (support vs marketing vs technical)
- [ ] **Tone matches audience** (parents vs educators vs partners)
- [ ] **No fear-based messaging** (positive, not manipulative)
- [ ] **Authentic and genuine** (not salesy or templated)

## Voice Evolution

### How Voice Adapts as Brand Grows

**Early Stage (Current):**
- More personal and intimate
- Direct communication with customers
- Story-driven (customer stories)
- **Voice:** Warm, personal, authentic

**Growth Stage:**
- More structured but still warm
- Consistent across channels
- Brand-driven (brand stories)
- **Voice:** Warm, expert, consistent

**Scale Stage:**
- Highly consistent
- Team-driven (multiple voices, one brand)
- Platform-driven (ecosystem stories)
- **Voice:** Warm, expert, innovative, safe (all four characteristics balanced)

### Voice Consistency

**Principles:**
- Core voice characteristics remain constant (warm, expert, innovative, safe)
- Tone adapts to context and audience
- Examples and stories evolve with brand
- **Consistency:** Voice stays the same, expression evolves

## Story Intelligence Voice

### Story Intelligence Specific Voice

**When talking about Story Intelligence™:**
- Always use the ™ symbol
- Capitalize both words
- Use as a proper noun
- **Voice:** Magical but credible, intelligent but accessible

**Example:**
- ✅ "Story Intelligence™ understands your child like a master storyteller would."
- ❌ "Our story intelligence technology creates personalized content." (not capitalized, no ™)

**Code References:**
- `docs/brand/01-story-intelligence-brand-guide.md:203-211` - Story Intelligence language guidelines

### Story Intelligence Messaging

**What We Say:**
- "Story Intelligence crafts award-caliber narratives"
- "Story Intelligence understands your child"
- "Powered by Story Intelligence"
- "Story Intelligence creates family treasures"

**What We Never Say:**
- "Story Intelligence™ powered" or "Story Intelligence™ enhanced"
- "AI-generated" or "machine learning"
- "Automated" or "algorithmic"
- "Personalized content" (too generic)
