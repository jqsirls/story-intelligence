## Content Creation Process

### Step 1: Research and Planning

**Research:**
- Understand audience needs
- Research topic thoroughly
- Identify key messages
- Review existing content (avoid duplication)

**Planning:**
- Define content objective
- Identify key messages
- Outline structure
- Determine call to action

### Step 2: Writing

**Writing Process:**
- Follow voice and tone guidelines
- Use messaging framework
- Structure content clearly
- Include examples and stories
- Write for audience (not internal)

### Step 3: Review

**Review Checklist:**
- Voice and tone appropriate
- Messaging aligns with framework
- Story Intelligence™ used correctly
- No jargon or technical terms unnecessarily
- Clear and understandable
- Accurate and factual
- No inappropriate content

### Step 4: Approval

**Approval Process:**
- Content creator self-review
- Peer review (if applicable)
- Marketing/content lead review
- Legal review (if sensitive)
- Final approval and publish

## Content Structure and Formatting

### Standard Structure

**Introduction:**
- Hook (capture attention)
- Value proposition (what's in it for reader)
- Overview (what they'll learn)

**Body:**
- Clear sections with headings
- Subheadings for organization
- Bullet points for lists
- Examples and stories
- Visual breaks (paragraphs, spacing)

**Conclusion:**
- Summary of key points
- Call to action (if applicable)
- Next steps or resources

### Formatting Guidelines

**Headings:**
- Use H2 for main sections
- Use H3 for subsections
- Use H4 for sub-subsections (if needed)
- Clear and descriptive headings

**Lists:**
- Use bullet points for unordered lists
- Use numbered lists for sequential steps
- Keep items concise
- Use parallel structure

**Paragraphs:**
- Keep paragraphs short (3-5 sentences)
- One idea per paragraph
- Use transitions between paragraphs

**Emphasis:**
- Use bold for key terms (sparingly)
- Use italics for emphasis (sparingly)
- Avoid all caps (unprofessional)

## SEO Guidelines

### For Blog Posts and Web Content

**Keyword Research:**
- Identify target keywords
- Use long-tail keywords (more specific)
- Consider search intent
- Balance keyword usage (not keyword stuffing)

**On-Page SEO:**
- Title tag: Include primary keyword, 50-60 characters
- Meta description: Include keyword, 150-160 characters
- Headings: Include keywords naturally
- URL structure: Clean, descriptive URLs
- Internal linking: Link to related content
- Image alt text: Descriptive, include keywords when relevant

**Content Quality:**
- Comprehensive content (thorough coverage)
- Original content (not duplicated)
- Valuable content (solves problems)
- Engaging content (keeps readers)

## Content Messaging

### Messaging Application

**Apply messaging framework to content:**
- Core themes (award-caliber quality, personalization, therapeutic value)
- Audience-specific messaging (parents, educators, therapists)
- Value propositions (primary, secondary, tertiary)

**Code References:**
- `docs/design/themes-and-messaging.md` - Complete messaging framework

### Message Hierarchy

**Level 1: Core Message (Always Lead With)**
- Award-caliber quality
- Personalization (Story Intelligence™)
- Therapeutic/developmental value

**Level 2: Supporting Messages (Reinforce Core)**
- Safety and compliance
- Multi-platform access
- Family bonding

**Level 3: Feature Messages (Details)**
- Specific features
- Technical capabilities
- Integration options

## Content Do's and Don'ts

### Do's

**✅ Do:**
- Use clear, simple language
- Focus on benefits to children and families
- Emphasize safety and quality
- Be authentic and genuine
- Use specific examples and stories
- Include Story Intelligence™ messaging (when relevant)
- Follow voice and tone guidelines
- Use messaging framework
- Proofread and edit carefully
- Get approval before publishing

### Don'ts

**❌ Don't:**
- Use jargon or technical terms unnecessarily
- Make promises we can't keep
- Ignore safety concerns
- Use fear-based messaging
- Sound salesy or pushy
- Use generic or templated language
- Compromise on quality messaging
- Skip review and approval
- Publish without proofreading
- Violate brand voice guidelines

## Content Metrics and Success Criteria

### Blog Post Metrics

**Engagement Metrics:**
- Page views
- Time on page
- Bounce rate
- Social shares
- Comments

**SEO Metrics:**
- Organic traffic
- Keyword rankings
- Backlinks
- Search impressions

**Conversion Metrics:**
- Click-through rate (CTR)
- Lead generation
- Sign-ups from content
- Revenue attribution

### Email Metrics

**Engagement Metrics:**
- Open rate (target: 20-30%)
- Click-through rate (target: 2-5%)
- Unsubscribe rate (target: < 0.5%)

**Conversion Metrics:**
- Conversion rate (target: 1-3%)
- Revenue per email
- Sign-ups from email

### Marketing Copy Metrics

**Engagement Metrics:**
- Click-through rate
- Time on page
- Scroll depth

**Conversion Metrics:**
- Conversion rate
- Sign-up rate
- Revenue attribution

### Success Criteria

**Content is Successful If:**
- Engages target audience (high engagement metrics)
- Drives conversions (sign-ups, revenue)
- Supports SEO goals (organic traffic)
- Maintains brand voice (consistent messaging)
- Provides value (solves problems, educates)
