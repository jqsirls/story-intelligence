# Mental Health Story Type - Marketing

**Last Updated**: 2025-01-15  
**Audience**: Marketing Team | Content Team | Brand  
**Story Type**: Mental Health  
**Age Range**: 3-12 years

## Value Proposition

Mental Health stories address emotions, feelings, and mental wellness through supportive narratives. These stories help children process emotions in healthy ways, learn coping strategies, and build emotional resilience and self-awareness.

## Target Audience

### Primary Users
- **Children Ages 4-12**: Children learning about emotions and feelings
- **Parents**: Supporting children's emotional development
- **Therapists**: Using stories in therapeutic settings
- **Educators**: Teaching emotional intelligence

## Key Features and Benefits

### Core Features
- **Emotional Processing**: Characters processing emotions in healthy ways
- **Coping Strategies**: Strategies for common childhood challenges
- **Emotional Validation**: Validation of feelings and experiences
- **Resilience Building**: Building emotional resilience and self-awareness
- **Professional Help**: Professional help portrayed positively when needed

### Benefits for Children
- Learn to process emotions healthily
- Develop coping strategies
- Build emotional resilience
- Increase self-awareness
- Understand that feelings are normal

## Use Cases

### Common Scenarios
1. **Emotional Support**: Supporting children with emotions
2. **Coping Skills**: Teaching coping strategies
3. **Therapeutic Support**: Supporting therapeutic interventions
4. **Emotional Intelligence**: Building emotional intelligence
5. **Mental Wellness**: Promoting mental wellness

## Messaging and Positioning

### Primary Message
"Support children's emotional well-being through stories that help them process emotions, learn coping strategies, and build emotional resilience."
