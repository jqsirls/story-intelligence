# Financial Literacy Story Type - Executives

**Last Updated**: 2025-01-15  
**Audience**: C-Suite | Executive Team | Board | Investors  
**Story Type**: Financial Literacy  
**Age Range**: 3-14 years

## Executive Summary

Financial Literacy stories address a growing market need for early financial education. These stories teach money management, saving, and financial responsibility through engaging narratives, positioning Storytailor in the financial education market.

**Key Metrics:**
- **Usage**: 5-10% of all story creations
- **Engagement**: 75%+ completion rate
- **Market Opportunity**: Growing financial education market
- **Revenue Impact**: Moderate driver for Family and Professional Tier subscriptions

## Business Value

### Revenue Impact
- **Family Tier**: Appeals to parents teaching financial literacy
- **Professional Tier**: Appeals to educators teaching financial concepts
- **Market Differentiation**: Unique approach to financial education

## Market Opportunity

### Total Addressable Market (TAM)
- **Financial Education Market**: $500M+ annually
- **Children's Financial Education**: Growing segment
- **EdTech Financial Tools**: Emerging market

### Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM)
- **Target Age Group**: 6-14 years (35M children in US)
- **Parent Interest**: 60% of parents want financial education for children
- **Addressable Families**: ~21M families

## Revenue Potential

### Direct Revenue
- **Family Tier**: $9.99/month × 8% of users = $0.80/month per user
- **Professional Tier**: $29.99/month × 2% of users = $0.60/month per user
- **Average Revenue Per User (ARPU)**: $1.50-2.00/month for financial literacy story users

### Revenue Projections
- **Year 1**: 2K-10K users × $1.75 ARPU × 12 months = $42K-$210K
- **Year 3**: 20K-100K users × $1.75 ARPU × 12 months = $420K-$2.1M
