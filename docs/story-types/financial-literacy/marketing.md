# Financial Literacy Story Type - Marketing

**Last Updated**: 2025-01-15  
**Audience**: Marketing Team | Content Team | Brand  
**Story Type**: Financial Literacy  
**Age Range**: 3-14 years

## Value Proposition

Financial Literacy stories teach money management, saving, and financial responsibility through engaging narratives. These age-appropriate stories introduce financial concepts in a fun, accessible way, building money-smart habits from an early age.

## Target Audience

### Primary Users
- **Children Ages 6-14**: Children learning about money
- **Parents**: Teaching financial literacy at home
- **Educators**: Teaching financial concepts in school
- **Financial Educators**: Using stories to teach money management

## Key Features and Benefits

### Core Features
- **Age-Appropriate Financial Concepts**: Saving, spending, sharing
- **Money-Smart Characters**: Characters making good money decisions
- **Needs vs. Wants**: Simple lessons about priorities
- **Value of Work**: Teaching about earning and work
- **Responsible Management**: Generosity and responsible money handling

### Benefits for Children
- Learn money management skills
- Understand saving and spending
- Learn about needs vs. wants
- Build financial responsibility
- Develop money-smart habits

## Use Cases

### Common Scenarios
1. **Teaching Saving**: Stories about saving money
2. **Allowance Education**: Teaching about allowance and money
3. **Financial Concepts**: Introducing banking, budgeting
4. **Money Values**: Teaching about generosity and sharing
5. **Work and Earning**: Stories about earning money

## Messaging and Positioning

### Primary Message
"Teach money management through engaging stories that make financial literacy fun and accessible, building money-smart habits from an early age."
