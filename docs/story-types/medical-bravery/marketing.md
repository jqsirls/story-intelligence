# Medical Bravery Story Type - Marketing

**Last Updated**: 2025-01-15  
**Audience**: Marketing Team | Content Team | Brand  
**Story Type**: Medical Bravery  
**Age Range**: 3-10 years

## Value Proposition

Medical Bravery stories help children cope with medical situations and build courage. These supportive stories feature characters facing medical procedures with bravery, providing accurate but non-scary medical information and coping strategies for medical anxiety.

## Target Audience

### Primary Users
- **Children Ages 3-10**: Children facing medical procedures
- **Parents**: Supporting children through medical experiences
- **Healthcare Providers**: Using stories to prepare children for procedures
- **Child Life Specialists**: Supporting children in medical settings

## Key Features and Benefits

### Core Features
- **Courage Building**: Characters facing medical procedures with bravery
- **Accurate Information**: Non-scary medical information
- **Coping Strategies**: Strategies for medical anxiety
- **Healthcare Heroes**: Healthcare workers portrayed as helpful heroes
- **Positive Outcomes**: Healing themes and positive outcomes

### Benefits for Children
- Builds courage for medical procedures
- Reduces medical anxiety
- Provides accurate medical information
- Teaches coping strategies
- Promotes positive medical experiences

### Benefits for Parents
- Supports children through medical experiences
- Reduces parent anxiety about procedures
- Provides tools for medical preparation
- Creates positive medical associations

## Use Cases

### Common Scenarios
1. **Medical Procedure Preparation**: Preparing children for doctor visits, surgeries
2. **Hospital Stays**: Supporting children during hospital stays
3. **Medical Anxiety**: Reducing anxiety about medical procedures
4. **Chronic Conditions**: Supporting children with ongoing medical needs
5. **Healthcare Education**: Teaching children about healthcare

## Messaging and Positioning

### Primary Message
"Help children build courage for medical procedures through supportive stories that provide accurate information, coping strategies, and positive medical experiences."
